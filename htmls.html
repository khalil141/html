Sergio Ramos

Position: Center Back (CB)

Name: Sergio Ramos García Position: Center Back (CB) Date of Birth: March 30, 1986 Nationality: Spanish Club Career: Ramos began his professional career at Sevilla FC, where he made his first-team debut in 2004 at the age of 18. In August 2005, he transferred to Real Madrid, one of the world's most prominent football clubs, for a then-record fee for a Spanish defender. Throughout his time at Real Madrid, Ramos became a key player and a club legend, playing a vital role in the team's success. Real Madrid: Ramos was an integral part of Real Madrid's golden era, during which the club achieved significant success in domestic and international competitions. He won numerous La Liga titles and Copa del Rey trophies with the club. Ramos played a crucial role in Real Madrid's remarkable run of winning four UEFA Champions League titles in five seasons from 2014 to 2018. Known for his defensive prowess, leadership skills, and goal-scoring ability, he became one of the most celebrated center-backs in football history. International Career: Ramos represented the Spanish national team at various levels, starting from the youth teams. He made his senior debut for Spain in 2005 and quickly established himself as a key member of the national squad. Ramos played a crucial role in Spain's success during the late 2000s and early 2010s, helping the team win the UEFA European Championship in 2008 and 2012 and the FIFA World Cup in 2010. Individual Achievements: Throughout his career, Ramos received numerous individual accolades, including multiple UEFA Team of the Year selections and FIFA FIFPro World XI nominations. He was known for his consistency, versatility, and ability to score crucial goals, particularly from set-pieces.
